To integrate perfectly with your Neo4j graph, the MongoDB schema should act as the "Source of Truth" for how your AI agents understand and classify your activities.
The strategy here is Polyglot Persistence: MongoDB handles the flexible, dynamic data (like evolving keyword lists and raw event descriptions), while Neo4j stores the structured relationships

1.The PillarClassifications Collection
This collection maps specific keywords found in your calendar or journal to your Life Pillars. 
It is what your agents will query before writing a :CLASSIFIED_AS relationship to Neo4j.

{
  "_id": ObjectId("..."),
  "pillar_name": "Health", 
  "keywords": [
    {"term": "gym", "confidence": 1.0, "source": "manual"},
    {"term": "cardio", "confidence": 0.9, "source": "ai_suggested"},
    {"term": "meal prep", "confidence": 0.85, "source": "ai_suggested"}
  ],
  "last_updated": "2024-04-29T14:00:00Z",
  "neo4j_node_id": "p_health_01" // Explicit link to the Pillar node in Neo4j
}

2. The ConfirmedActuals Collection
This stores the "Actual" events that have been confirmed by you. By storing the full JSON here, you keep Neo4j lean—only passing the essential relationship data to the graph.

{
  "_id": ObjectId("..."),
  "time_chunk_id": "2024-W18-D1-C3", // Maps to the TimeChunk in Neo4j
  "raw_input": "Finished the heavy lifting session and felt great.",
  "extracted_keywords": ["lifting", "gym"],
  "assigned_pillars": ["Health"],
  "human_confirmed": true,
  "metadata": {
    "is_valuable_detour": false,
    "matches_intent": true
  }
}

How they work together (The Sync Loop)
AI Detection: When you post to your /Journal endpoint, the agent queries the PillarClassifications collection in MongoDB to find matching keywords.
Graph Update: The agent creates the (:Actual) node in Neo4j and draws a :RECORDED_AS relationship from the correct (:TimeChunk).
Human Feedback: If you tell the agent, "Actually, 'meal prep' is also part of my 'Wealth' pillar (saving money)," the agent updates the PillarClassifications document in MongoDB and adds a new :CLASSIFIED_AS relationship in Neo4j.
